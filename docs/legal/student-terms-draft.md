# KAXI Student Terms Draft

Status: draft for legal review
Last checked: 2026-07-01
Review status: needs_legal_review

## 1. Purpose

These terms govern use of KAXI, a platform that helps prospective international students prepare for study in Korea through school search, cost comparison, document checklists, official-source AI guidance, and referral to independent partners.

## 2. Service Scope

KAXI may provide:

- general information about D-2/D-4 study preparation based on official sources;
- school, tuition, dormitory, accreditation, and cost comparison tools;
- AI-generated document checklists and preparation reminders;
- risk flags for fake documents, illegal employment, visa guarantee claims, or complex immigration history;
- referral/intake routing to licensed administrative-scrivener, translation, admission, settlement, or related partners.

## 3. What KAXI Does Not Provide

KAXI does not:

- guarantee visa issuance, admission, residence status approval, job placement, or any administrative outcome;
- act as an administrative scrivener, attorney, immigration officer, school, embassy, or government agency;
- prepare final documents for submission to an administrative agency or submit documents on behalf of a user unless performed by a separately engaged licensed partner;
- provide illegal employment matching, fake document support, visa guarantee brokerage, or advice to evade immigration rules.

## 4. AI Guidance

AI outputs are general guidance generated from KAXI data, official-source RAG documents, and user-provided context. AI answers may be incomplete or outdated. Users must verify requirements with the relevant school, embassy/consulate, immigration office, or a licensed administrative-scrivener partner before filing.

KAXI may add a source notice such as:

> 이 안내는 2026-07-01에 확인된 Study in Korea / 법무부 출처 기준입니다. 개인 상황에 따라 달라질 수 있어 접수 전 행정사 검토가 필요합니다.

## 5. Partner Services

Partners are independent service providers. If a user requests paid administrative-scrivener work, document drafting, filing representation, or case-specific consultation, a separate partner engagement, fee notice, consent, and mandate may be required. KAXI may transmit intake information to the selected partner only with a valid notice/consent basis.

## 6. User Responsibilities

Users must:

- provide accurate and lawful information;
- not upload or request fake, altered, stolen, or misleading documents;
- not request illegal work placement or visa approval guarantees;
- independently confirm deadlines and required documents before submission;
- keep passport, bank, school, and immigration documents secure.

## 7. Prohibited Conduct

KAXI may reject, block, or terminate use when a user requests or attempts:

- fake or altered documents;
- illegal employment, unlicensed job brokerage, or work without required permission;
- visa guarantee schemes;
- agency submission or representation through KAXI rather than an authorized partner;
- scraping, attacking, reverse engineering, or abusing AI/rate-limit systems;
- disclosure of another person's personal information without authority.

## 8. Fees and Refunds

KAXI platform fees, if any, must be shown before payment. Partner fees are separate unless expressly bundled and disclosed. Refund terms must be presented at purchase and may differ for platform software, partner consultation, translation, or other services.

## 9. Privacy

KAXI processes personal information under the KAXI Privacy Policy. Sensitive free-text should be minimized. Partner routing may involve third-party provision or processing consignment depending on the flow.

## 10. Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, KAXI is not responsible for decisions by schools, embassies, immigration offices, government agencies, or independent partners. Nothing in these terms limits liability that cannot be limited by applicable law.

## 11. Changes

KAXI may update these terms. Material changes affecting user rights or partner data flow should be notified in advance where required.
