# Administrative-Scrivener Partner Contract Structure Draft

Status: draft for legal review
Last checked: 2026-07-01
Review status: needs_legal_review

This is a structure draft for contracts with licensed administrative-scrivener partners. It is not the final agreement.

## 1. Parties

- Platform operator: KAXI
- Partner: licensed administrative scrivener or administrative-scrivener office
- User/student: end user who may enter a separate engagement with the partner

## 2. Licensing and Eligibility

Partner must represent and warrant that:

- partner holds and maintains all required administrative-scrivener licenses/registrations;
- partner will not delegate licensed work to unqualified persons;
- partner will notify KAXI immediately of suspension, disciplinary action, license issue, conflict of interest, or inability to perform.

KAXI may periodically request license evidence and remove partner access if evidence is missing or outdated.

## 3. Scope Split

KAXI provides:

- software platform, intake, routing, scheduling, records, and payment support if enabled;
- official-source general AI guidance;
- non-licensed school/cost/document checklist tools.

Partner provides only under a separate user engagement where required:

- case-specific visa/residence assessment;
- administrative-agency submission document drafting;
- proxy submission or communication where legally permitted;
- refusal/appeal/objection consultation;
- final review before filing.

## 4. No Visa Guarantee

Partner must not claim or imply guaranteed visa issuance, guaranteed admission, guaranteed employment, false documents, or special influence over schools, embassies, immigration offices, or public officials.

## 5. User Engagement and Fees

The contract should define:

- whether KAXI only introduces the partner or also processes payment;
- when the partner's separate mandate/engagement starts;
- fee disclosure, tax invoice/receipt responsibility, refund/cancellation rules;
- service deliverables, response times, and consultation channel;
- who owns and stores final submission documents.

## 6. Data Protection

Partner must:

- use user data only for the requested consultation/service;
- keep confidentiality;
- implement technical and managerial safeguards;
- delete/return data when purpose ends unless legal retention applies;
- avoid onward transfer without KAXI/user authorization;
- report breach or suspected misuse immediately.

The data-flow classification must be decided per operation:

- third-party provision when KAXI transfers lead data to an independent partner for the partner's own service relationship;
- processing consignment only where the partner acts strictly on KAXI's behalf and not as an independent professional service provider.

## 7. Quality and Review

Partner should maintain:

- source-based advice;
- written case notes for paid consultations;
- escalation for uncertain or high-risk matters;
- no use of KAXI AI output as final legal/administrative judgment without professional review.

## 8. Compliance and Prohibited Conduct

Prohibited:

- fake documents or document alteration;
- illegal employment placement or unlicensed job brokerage;
- visa guarantee advertising;
- misrepresenting KAXI as a government agency, embassy, school, or law/administrative office;
- using user data for unrelated marketing without consent;
- bypassing KAXI consent and audit processes for leads received from KAXI.

## 9. Audit, Suspension, and Termination

KAXI may suspend routing for:

- license evidence failure;
- user complaints or repeated SLA failure;
- suspected privacy breach;
- guarantee/fake-document/illegal-work claims;
- regulatory or reputational risk.

## 10. Liability and Indemnity

Draft allocation:

- KAXI is responsible for platform operation, accurate routing records, and privacy obligations within KAXI-controlled systems.
- Partner is responsible for professional advice, licensed work, user engagement terms, professional negligence, and partner-side privacy/security obligations.
- Final allocation must be reviewed by counsel and reflected in insurance requirements.
